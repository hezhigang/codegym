Extended Euclidean algorithm
Euclid's algorithm also gives us more: We can extend it so that it will compute integers a and b satisfying am+ bn = gcd(m, n) (4.5) If m = 0, we simply take a = 0 and b = 1. Otherwise we let r = n mod m and apply the method recursively with r and m in place of m and n, computing r and m such that a'r + b'm = gcd(r, m) hence a=b'-Math.floor(n/m)*a' and b=a'
